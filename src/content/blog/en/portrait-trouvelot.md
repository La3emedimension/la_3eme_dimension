# Étienne Léopold Trouvelot (1827-1895)
## The master of astronomical illustration

Étienne Léopold Trouvelot, French artist and astronomer who emigrated to the United States, revolutionized the art of astronomical illustration. His spectacular pastels of celestial phenomena remain unequaled for their beauty and scientific precision.

## His unique art

- **Mixed technique**: Pastel, chalk and charcoal to capture celestial nuances
- **Scientific precision**: Every detail was carefully observed and verified
- **Remarkable aesthetics**: His works are true artistic masterpieces
- **Innovation**: First to represent auroras borealis with such realism

## Iconic works

Trouvelot created more than 7000 astronomical illustrations, including his famous representations of:
- Sunspots and prominences
- The Orion Nebula
- Auroras borealis
- Detailed lunar phases

## Working method

He observed from Harvard Observatory, using high-quality telescopes. Each illustration required several nights of observation to capture all the details and variations.

## Artistic legacy

His works permanently influenced astronomical iconography. They still adorn many scientific works today and inspire contemporary artists.

## Recognition

His illustrations were published by Harvard Observatory and exhibited worldwide, establishing a new standard for astronomical art.
